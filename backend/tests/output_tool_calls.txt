Web search results for "what is quantum superposition":

**Summary** (Wikipedia):
Quantum superposition is a fundamental principle of quantum mechanics. It states that, much like waves in classical physics, any two quantum states can be added together and the result will be another valid quantum state; and conversely, that every quantum state can be represented as a sum of two or more other distinct states. Mathematically, it refers to a property of solutions to the Schrödinger equation; since the Schrödinger equation is linear, any linear combination of solutions will also be a solution.

Context: This simulation demonstrates the single-electron double-slit experiment — the quintessential demonstration of wave-particle duality in quantum mechanics. Electrons are fired one at a time through a pair of slits. Each electron arrives as a localized dot on the detector screen (particle behavior), yet after many electrons accumulate, the classic interference fringe pattern emerges (wave behavior). The purpose is to make tangible the idea that quantum objects do not travel along definite paths but instead exhibit superposition of all possible paths until measurement.
Visuals: The main canvas shows two vertical slits in a barrier with electrons emitted from a source on the left. Each electron traverses the apparatus and lands as a single bright dot on the detector screen to the right. As hundreds of electrons accumulate, the dots collectively form the characteristic bright and dark interference bands. A combined chart below the main canvas overlays the growing histogram of electron hits against the theoretical interference envelope. When the 'Show Classical Pattern' button is activated, a smooth classical prediction curve appears for comparison. A guided narration panel at the bottom walks through the physics step by step with play/pause/back controls and a progress bar.

  slit_width: Slider (100–500 units). Controls the width of each individual slit.
  wavelength: Slider (380–700 nm). Sets the de Broglie wavelength of the electrons, also changes the dot color to match.
  screen_distance: Slider (4000–8000 units). Moves the detector screen closer to or farther from the slits.
  emission_rate: Slider (5–30 electrons/sec). Controls how quickly electrons are fired.
  show_classical_pattern: Toggle button. Overlays the classical (no-interference) prediction on the detection chart.
