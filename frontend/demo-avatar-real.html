Navier-Stokes Equations

The Navier-Stokes equations describe how fluids move. They're Newton's second law applied to fluid elements — acceleration equals the sum of forces.

The pressure gradient pushes fluid from high to low pressure. Viscosity acts like friction between fluid layers. External forces like gravity pull everything down.
