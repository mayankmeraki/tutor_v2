The Idea
Walk through. At each node, flip the arrow backwards.
Three pointers:
1
prev — where we just were
2
current — where we are now
3
next — saved before we flip
Pythonreverse.py
 1def reverse(head):
 2    prev = None
 3    curr = head
 4    while curr:
 5        nxt = curr.next   # save
 6        curr.next = prev  # flip
 7        prev = curr        # advance
 8        curr = nxt         # advance
 9    return prev
TimeO(n)
SpaceO(1)
"Save next before you flip — or you lose your way forward."

Water = min(height[i], height[j]) × (j - i)
= min(1, 7) × 8 = 8
The Key Insight
Move the shorter side inward. Why? Because moving the taller side can only make it worse or the same — the water level is capped by the short side.
Python
1def max_water(height):
2    i, j = 0, len(height) - 1
3    best = 0
4    while i < j:
5        water = min(height[i], height[j]) * (j - i)
6        best = max(best, water)
7        if height[i] < height[j]:
8            i += 1  # move shorter side
9        else:
10            j -= 1
11    return best
TimeO(n)
SpaceO(1)
"Always move the shorter side. The tall side can't help until the short side catches up."

Python
1def inorder(node):
2    if not node: return []
3    return inorder(node.left) + [node.val] + inorder(node.right)
Why does inorder give sorted?
BST property: everything left is smaller, everything right is bigger. Inorder visits left first, then root, then right — that's small → medium → large.
