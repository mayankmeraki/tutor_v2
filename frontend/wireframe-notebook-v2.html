Shared Blackboard — Interaction Model

Three chalk colors = three voices

  • White chalk — tutor's equations (the derivation steps)
  • Blue chalk — tutor's words (hints, nudges, feedback, corrections)
  • Green chalk — student's work (typed math + canvas drawings)
  • Yellow chalk — step labels (both tutor and student)

What the tutor can do

  • Write a step — equation in white, label in yellow (existing tag)
  • Write freely — blue chalk text, anywhere in the flow (hints, nudges, praise, explanations)
  • Write a correction — blue chalk equation, clearly from the tutor, not the student

What the student can do

  • Draw + type in the workspace, submit. Appears on the board in green.
  • Try again after a hint. New attempt appears below, both visible.

Philosophy

  • No crossing out, no red marks — everything stays visible as a learning trail
  • Mistakes + corrections both on the board — the journey IS the lesson
  • Chat sidebar handles conversation. Board is purely for the math.
