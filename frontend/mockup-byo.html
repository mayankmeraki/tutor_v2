SN1 vs SN2
The two paths for nucleophilic substitution
From your textbook (p. 78):
"The choice between SN1 and SN2 depends on the substrate, nucleophile, solvent, and leaving group."
— Organic Chemistry, Ch. 6, p. 78
The Decision Tree
1. Look at the substrate carbon:
• Primary (1°) → SN2 (almost always)
• Secondary (2°) → depends on nucleophile + solvent
• Tertiary (3°) → SN1 (almost always)

This topic appeared in 4 out of 5 past exams. Questions 3 and 4 in 2024 were both SN1/SN2.
