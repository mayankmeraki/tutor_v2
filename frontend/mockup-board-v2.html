Mass–Energy Equivalence
the most famous equation in physics
E = mc²
energy equals mass times the speed of light squared
What it says
Mass and energy are the same thing, measured in different units. A tiny amount of mass contains an enormous amount of energy because c² is huge.
How huge is c²?
c
3 × 10⁸ m/s
c²
9 × 10¹⁶ m²/s²
That's 90,000,000,000,000,000
Concrete example
1 kg of matter, fully converted to energy:
E = 1 × (3×10⁸)²
E = 9 × 10¹⁶ J
≈ 21 megatons of TNT
The point
A paperclip (1g) contains enough energy to power a city for a day. Mass IS energy, just locked up.
Backpropagation
how neural networks learn from mistakes
Input
→forward
Hidden layers
→forward
Prediction
→compare
Loss
Forward pass
Data flows left to right through the network. Each layer transforms it. The final layer makes a prediction.
input → weights × input + bias → activation → next layer
Backward pass
The loss signal flows RIGHT TO LEFT. Each weight learns: "this is how much YOU contributed to the error — adjust by this much."
∂Loss/∂w = how much w contributed to the mistake
The chain rule makes it possible
∂L/∂w₁ = ∂L/∂a₃ · ∂a₃/∂a₂ · ∂a₂/∂w₁
blame flows backward through each layer via multiplication
One sentence
Forward = predict. Loss = how wrong. Backward = blame each weight. Update = nudge weights to be less wrong next time.
