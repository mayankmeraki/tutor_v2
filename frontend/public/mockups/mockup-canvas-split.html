Lesson · the punchline

Two coils, one shared flux.

EQ 2
Vs  ⁄  Vp  =  Ns  ⁄  Np

Both coils feel the same changing flux Φ(t). Faraday tells us each turn picks up the same EMF — so a coil with more turns stacks more voltage. The ratio of voltages just equals the ratio of turns.

⚡ key insight Voltage doesn't care about wire thickness, current, or how heavy the brick feels. It cares only about turns and the rate of flux change.
